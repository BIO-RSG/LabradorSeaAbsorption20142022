# Quantifying shifts in the absorption budget of the Labrador Sea as phytoplankton communities switch between diatom and *Phaeocystis* dominated

## This repository contains data, code, and accompanying figures and tables for:

*Wilson KL, Devred E, Perry T and Clay S (2026) Quantifying shifts in the absorption budget of the Labrador Sea as phytoplankton communities switch between diatom and Phaeocystis dominated. Journal of Geophysical Research: Oceans*

Who analyzed the phytoplankton, non-algal particulate (NAP, detritus), and colour dissolved organic material (CDOM) absorption from 2014 to 2022 in the Labrador Sea in relationship to two large *Phaeocystis* blooms.

## This paper is a sister paper to:

*Devred E., Wilson K. L., Perry T., Hardy M., Brosnahan M., and Ringuette M. 2024. Identification and validation of phytoplankton taxonomic assemblages derived from pigment signatures using samples collected in the Labrador Sea from 2014 to 2022. Can. Tech. Rep. Fish. Aquat. Sci. 3596:viii + 55 p*

Who analyzed seven years of High Performance Liquid Chromatography (HPLC) data with Phytoclass to compute the phytoplankton assemblages in the Labrador Sea. The Phytoclass results were validated against three different measurements of in situ phytoplankton measurements.

**And:**

*Devred E., Clay S., Ringuette M., Perry T., Amirian M., Irwin A., and Finkel Z. (2025) Net primary production in the Labrador Sea between 2014 and 2022 derived from ocean colour remote sensing based on ecological regimes. Remote Sensing of Environment 323:114713.*

Who defined four oceanographic regimes in the Labrador Sea, and used these regimes to classify satellite based primary production from 2014 to 2022.
